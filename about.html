About

Meet Adero.

Founder of Adero Consultancy, Adero works at the intersection of design, engineering, and belonging — helping students see accessibility not as a compliance checkbox, but as one of the most useful skills they'll ever learn.

The Story

A career built on curiosity, not a straight line.

Adero's path has never followed a single track — from a technical grounding that stretches into biomedical engineering, to consulting, to the stage. That range is the point: when a student asks how any of it connects, the honest answer is that it doesn't have to, as long as you keep showing up and keep learning.

Bilingual and unapologetically direct, Adero built Adero Consultancy to bring that same range into classrooms — proof that expertise in one field doesn't disqualify you from speaking with authority in another, and that confidence is a skill you can practice like any other.

The Belief

"Kind of delulu" — and proud of it.

Every workshop carries the same undercurrent: belief in yourself has to come before the evidence does. Adero calls it being "kind of delulu" — the willingness to commit to a goal before the world has proven it's possible. It's the same belief required to start a business, learn a new language, or design for a body different from your own.

Students remember the realness more than the resume. That's intentional.

Approach

Three things every session is built on.

Empathy as method

Accessibility starts by feeling a barrier, not just being told about one. Students test real constraints before they redesign anything.

Range over resume

Adero draws on engineering, business, and bilingual experience to show students that expertise is built, not inherited.

Confidence as curriculum

Belief in yourself gets taught alongside the technical content — because neither one works without the other.
