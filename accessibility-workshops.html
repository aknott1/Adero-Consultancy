Accessibility Workshops

Building empathetic students one workshop at a time.

Interactive sessions that teach accessibility as a design discipline — for middle and high schoolers in private and public schools alike. Students leave able to spot a barrier, explain why it matters, and redesign around it.

Grades 6–12 Half-day / full-day / multi-week In-person or virtual Private & public schools
Sample Curriculum

What a typical engagement covers.

01

Foundations of Disability and Inclusive Design/h3>

What accessibility means beyond ramps and captions — the principle that the more people who can use something, the better it is, for everyone.

02

Barrier Walkthroughs

Students experience common digital and physical barriers firsthand using guided simulations, then map where and why systems fail.

03

Assistive Technology in Action

A hands-on look at the tools people rely on daily, and how good design anticipates them instead of retrofitting for them.

04

Redesign Sprint

Small teams take a real product, app, or space and redesign it for accessibility — presenting their fixes to the group.

05

Confidence & Career Talk

Adero closes with an honest conversation about building a career on curiosity — including how a technical background outside your field can still shape your thinking.

Formats

Flexible enough for any school calendar.

Half-day intensive — a single deep-dive session for one class or grade level, ideal for advisory periods or elective blocks.

Full-day workshop — the complete curriculum with team redesign challenges, built for a dedicated programming day.

Multi-week residency — Adero returns across several weeks, pairing with teachers to embed accessibility into an existing course or club.

Outcomes schools tell us matter most

Students can name and explain the core principles of accessible design, articulate why inclusive systems perform better for everyone, and apply a repeatable framework to a real design problem — not just recall a lecture.

Educators receive a short recap and framework handout to reinforce the material after Adero leaves.
